Section 8

Implementation Plan

This section provides an understanding of how to implement the project in practice. It must be clear how the community is involved at every stage — from construction through to long-term ownership.

  • Stage 1 — Construction & Community Training

    How will the solution be constructed? Who will build it? What training is required for community members to participate in construction and to use the solution day-to-day? [Approx. 2–4 sentences.]

  • Stage 2 — Introduction to Client & Community

    How will the solution be introduced to the client or community? What does the rollout process look like in the short term? How will uptake be encouraged? [Approx. 2–4 sentences.]

  • Stage 3 — Long-term Use, Management & Maintenance

    How will the solution be managed and maintained over time? Who is responsible? What resources are required? What is the expected service life? [Approx. 2–4 sentences.]

  • Stage 4 — Evaluation

    How will the design be evaluated once implemented? What indicators of success will be tracked? Who conducts the evaluation, and over what time frame? [2–4 sentences.]

  • Stage 5 — Community Ownership

    How can the client or community take full ownership of the project? What handover process is envisaged? How does the design reduce dependency on external expertise or funding? [2–4 sentences.]

  • Stage 6 — Repair & Contingency

    How will the solution be repaired if something goes wrong? Are replacement parts locally available? What contingency plans exist for common failure modes? [2–3 sentences.]

Future Impact on the Community
